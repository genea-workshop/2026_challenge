Challenge overview

The challenge is intended for researchers working on speech-driven gesture generation and related non-verbal behaviour modelling. Its goal is to make comparisons between approaches more meaningful by centering the task around shared materials, a common schedule, and a publication track.

The 2026 timeline includes the challenge launch, release of test sequences, submission of test movements, release of evaluation results, and the paper submission and review cycle. This page can be extended with additional challenge materials and submission instructions as they become available.

For background on the broader series, see the earlier GENEA Challenge pages linked above and the main GENEA site at genea-workshop.github.io.

Call for participation

We invite participation from academia and industry on methods for speech-driven gesture generation and evaluation. GENEA Challenge 2026 is intended as a shared point of comparison for systems that generate embodied non-verbal behaviour from speech and related inputs.

Participants should track the full 2026 schedule: April 12, 2026 for the challenge launch, June 21, 2026 for test sequence release, June 28, 2026 for test movement submission, July 10, 2026 for evaluation results, July 15, 2026 for paper submission, August 3, 2026 for author notifications, and August 8, 2026 for the camera-ready deadline.

Additional rules, data access details, and submission instructions can be added here as the 2026 challenge materials are released.
